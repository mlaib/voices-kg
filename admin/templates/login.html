What this resource lets you do

  • Browse 982 Holocaust survivor testimonies as structured RDF events with participants, locations, time intervals, activities, and emotional annotations.
  • Surface narrative patterns across the corpus — how emotional register varies by life period, what themes dominate the camp years, which place names cluster around historical events.
  • Run SPARQL across seven named graphs, with outward skos:exactMatch alignments to GeoNames and Wikidata for federated queries.
  • Search 647,455 transcript segments full-text via Meilisearch and jump from any hit to its full interview context, including the Vonnegut-style emotion arc.

Abstract

Holocaust archives preserve hundreds of thousands of audio-video interviews with survivors of Nazi persecutions. These testimonies are multi-modal artefacts — video recordings, time-aligned transcripts, and emotions expressed verbally and physiologically during the interview process — yet no existing Semantic Web resource jointly represents their narrative structure, emotional content, and audiovisual dimension. VOICES addresses this gap with three contributions: an OWL 2 ontology that reuses CIDOC-CRM, OWL-Time, MFOEM, the W3C Media Ontology, and the Web Annotation Data Model; a knowledge-graph construction methodology that extracts narrative events from transcripts and aligns them outward to Wikidata and GeoNames; and a populated knowledge graph that connects testimony events to the Linked Open Data ecosystem.
